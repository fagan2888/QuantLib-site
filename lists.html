Lists of interest for financial quantitative analysts

This page is an attempt at a comprehensive list of mailing lists, newsgroups and forums of interest for financial quantitative analysts, besides the QuantLib mailing lists.

The list is intended to include anything where you can discuss/submit:

  • research papers (focused on quantitative issues)
  • modelling approaches (mono/multi factor models, trees, exotic options, etc.)
  • math issues (linear algebra, multidimensional solver, etc)
  • programming languages (C++/Java/etc.)
  • software products (applications, libraries, etc.)
  • info providers (news, historical and real time data, etc.)

This does not claim to be exhaustive: if you can suggest other related area or you want to contribute other references please email us with any of the following:

  • description;
  • web page (only if they are discussion list related);
  • FAQ file;
  • email address;
  • newsgroup name;
  • how to subscribe;
  • anything useful.

Your feedback is always welcome.

  • All About Derivatives

    • Type: Yahoo Club
    • Description: This club is intended for finance professionals to discuss the pricing and risk management of options, futures, swaps, swaptions, structured notes, callable bonds, exotic options, VAR, credit risk, and other topics related to derivative securities. Assisting other members with their research or problems, both scholarly and applied, is encouraged.
    • Subscribe: as for all Yahoo clubs

  • comp-finance

    • Type: Yahoo Groups mailing list
    • Description: Computational Finance and Trading Systems. A rather broad field, including many diverse areas of applied mathematics, computer science and economics. The two distinguishing characteristics are: 1) Financial markets, 2) Computer technology.
    • Subscribe: web form

  • Econophysics

    • Type: mailing list
    • Description: Reseachers with an interest in Econophysics can now receive regular email alerts via the CONTENTS-Alert e-mail service from Elsevier Science. This service delivers at regular intervals subject-clustered tables of contents from the journal Physica A, highlighted on the Econophysics web site.
    • Subscribe: web form

  • finance-and-physics

    • Type: mailing list
    • Description: Application of methods of statistical physics and non-linear dynamics to micro/macro-economic modelling and financial market analysis. This will benefit the research community and particularly will help to set up a UK based academic network in the field.
    • Subscribe: web form

  • Financial Risk Management Discussion Group

    • Type: online forum
    • Description: Read the latest news or swap ideas with risk management professionals from around the world. The Financial Risk Management Discussion Group is your chance to get involved, so post a message, ask a question or respond to someone else's posting.
      The forum is hosted by Glyn Holton's Contingency Analysis site.
    • Subscribe: does not apply.

  • MathFinance Newsletter

    • Type: newsletter
    • Description: The MathFinance Newsletter is a free service by http://www.mathfinance.de.
      It has been founded in November 1999 and contains topics such as new papers, books, websites, job exchange for quants, students, interns, executives, and researchers, events, questions and answers in financial engineering, and contact search. If you wish to place a question or answer, a job advertisement, recommend a book, a paper, a website or an event, please send mail to the technical editor.
    • Subscribe: send a message to newsletter@mathfinance.de

  • Object-Oriented Numerics List

    • Type: mailing list
    • Description: The Object-Oriented Numerics List (oon-list) is a forum for discussing scientific computing in object-oriented languages. There are currently some 1000 subscribers from academia, industry and government organizations around the world. Message topics include design, implementation, and performance of object-oriented numerical codes; discussion of language issues; project, code, and book announcements; paper abstracts and bibliography entries.
    • Subscribe: web form

  • oonstd

    • Type: mailing list
    • Description: a forum for discussing possible standard interfaces for object-oriented scientific computing components. To keep the content focused, the list will be restricted to library developers.
    • Subscribe: web form

  • Python-Finance

    • Type: Yahoo Groups mailing list
    • Description: for discussing how Python is used in the financial community, and developing tools to enhance its utility there.
    • Subscribe: web form

  • QuantitativeFinance

    • Type: Yahoo Groups mailing list
    • Description: This discussion group addresses quantitative methodologies and their software implementation in day-to-day trading, financing, valuation, and risk managament situations. This is a forum where both practical issues such as market conventions or spreadsheet problems can be discussed, or where views on more complex issues such as interest and credit models and their use in the valuation and risk management of exotic options, structured notes, or complete trading books can be exchanged. The group furthermore aims to promote a stronger link between the practitioner, the analyst, and the developer in specific, and the (applied) research community as a whole.
    • Subscribe: web form


  • Risk Management Discussion Forums

    • Type: online forum(s)
    • Description: The risk forums allow financial risk management professionals to pose questions and answer questions asked by other members. Topics: Credit/Liquidity Risk, Energy Risk, FRM Exam Discussion, General Risk Issues, Market Specific Risk, Questions & Answers Forum, Technology, What's Happening. Guests are limited to read-only access, GARP members have posting privilege.
    • Subscribe: does not apply.

  • Risk Talk

    • Type: online forum
    • Description: Risk Talk is RiskMetrics' Risk Management forum, where our users and clients voice their opinions and compare notes. It is used as a technical conferencing area by clients, students, and other risk management professionals. Guests are limited to read-only access, members have posting privilege.
    • Subscribe: does not apply.

  • Risk Theory Forum

    • Type: Delphi.com online forum
    • Description: General risk theory and its applications in financial and actuarial areas. Includes risk measurement and management, portfolio theory, rational decision-making under uncertainty.
    • Subscribe: you have to get a Delphi.com account.

  • Value at Risk Resources Forum

    • Type: Delphi.com online forum
    • Description: the GloriaMundi Forum is for those with questions about VaR and risk management in general.
    • Subscribe: you have to get a Delphi.com account.

  • Wilmott Forum

    • Type: online forum(s)
    • Description: There are three forums:
      • General Forum: for general Quantitative Finance banter
      • Technical Forum: whether you're a beginner or a seasoned professional this is the place for your techno-babble.
      • Jobs Forum: Tense nervous headache? Track down a new job here
    • Subscribe: you have to get an account at Wilmott.com

  • Wilmott Research Report

    • Type: newsletter
    • Description: Wilmott Research Report is published every two weeks and it provides, in easily digestible form, a brief rundown of what's been going on Wilmott.com, as well as providing new articles and a review of working papers currently available on the web.
    • Subscribe: you have to be a registered user at Wilmott.com
